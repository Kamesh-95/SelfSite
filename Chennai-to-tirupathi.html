Tirupathi

Tirupati is a pilgrimage city located in the vicinity of the Tirupati Temple, Chittoor District, Andhra Pradesh, India. Its Sri Venkateswara Temple sits atop one of the the 7 peaks of Tirumala Hills, attracting scores of Hindu pilgrims.

Hence Andhra receives its most popular and vibrant side as a religious destination.

Tirumala Temple

Tirumala is reputed as the most ancient temple in India.

This Temple is dedicated to Lord Sri Venkateswara, an incarnation of Vishnu, who is believed to have appeared here to save mankind from trials and troubles of Kali Yuga.

Situated on the top of lush green hills, this place attracts many tourists and religious pilgrims.

 

Sri Padmavathi Ammavari Temple

Padmavathi Temple is the temple dedicated to Goddess Padmavathi or Alamelumanga, the consort of Lord Venkateswara.

It is said that the Mother Goddess manifested herself in the holy Pushkarini calledPadmasarovaram in a golden lotus.

Lakhs of devotees take a dip in the holy water during Padmavathi Brahmotsavams every year.


 

Sri Govindarajaswami Temple

The temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, who is referred to as Govindarajaswami, was built during 12 century and was consecrated in the year 1130 AD by Saint Ramanujacharya.

The Tirupati city is built around this temple.

The deity will be in reclining yoga nidra posture, facing east, by keeping right hand under his head and left hand straight over his body with his two concerts in sitting posture at the foot of Govindaraja.


 

Papavinasanam

Situated around 5 Km from the main Tirumala temple, is an important sighting location with beautiful waterfalls.

It is believed that the holy waters of this place dissolve the sins of devotees.

Hence it is called as Papavinasanam. It is said that water comes from the feet of Lord Vishnu.


 

Srinivasa Mangapuram

Situated at 12 Km to the west of Tirupathi, this place serves as a home to Sri Kalyana Venkateswara Swamy Temple, which is considered as an alternative to those devotees who are unable to visit the Balaji temple on Tirumala Hills.

According to the Mythology, Lord Venkateswara stayed here after his wedding with Goddess Sri Padmavathi Devi before proceeding to Tirumala.

It is also visited by many newly-wed couples to seek lord’s blessing for a happy married life.

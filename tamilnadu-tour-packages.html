Tamilnadu –

Tamil Nadu is the homeland of one of humanity’s living classical civilisations with an ancient history that speaks for many ages.

It has many natural resources, historic buildings and religious sites including Hindu temples of Tamil architecture, hill stations, beach resorts, multi-religious pilgrimage sites, and eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Ooty –

Ooty or Udhagamandalam,the Queen of hill stations, is an important tourist destination in Tamil Nadu.

Lofty mountains, dense forest, sprawling grasslands and miles and miles of tea gardens greet you once you enter into its provinces.

Major attractions: Nilgiri Mountain Railway, Ooty Botanical Garden, Ooty Lake, Rose Garden, Doddabetta Peak, Mudhumalai National Park etc...

 

Kodaikanal –

Kodaikanal is a city in the hills of the Dindigul district in the state of Tamil Nadu.

It is the most sought after honeymoon destination in South India because of its scenic beauty with which it can create the most memorable experiences for its visitors.

To make the most of the enchanting surroundings one can undertake various activities such as trekking, boating, horse riding etc.

Major attractions: Kodai Lake, Green Valley View, Pillar Lake, Coakers Walk, Dolphin’s Nose, Bryant Park, Kodaikanal Solar Observatory etc...


 

Kanyakumari –

Located at the southernmost part of India, Kanyakumari with its beautiful beaches, Vivekananda Memorial and the perfect sunset is one of the most exotic places to go in India.

Possibly amongst one of the most exotic destinations in India, you can find temples, churches, religious pillars and statues everywhere to keep you fascinated.

Major attractions: Vivekananda Rock Memorial, Thiruvalluvar Statue, Kanyakumari Beach, Gandhi Memorial Mandapam, Kumari Amman Temple, Padmanabhapuram Palace, Wax Museum etc....

 

Madurai –

One of South India’s great temple cities, Madurai is synonymous with the celebrated Meenakshi Temple.

Situated on the banks of river Vaigai, Madurai has a rich cultural heritage passed on from the great Tamil era .

Popularly called as Thoonga Nagaram meaning the city that never sleeps, on account of the active night life.

Major attractions: Meenakshi Amman Temple, Aayiram Kaal Mandapam, Thirumala Nayakkar Mahal, kazimar big mosque and maqbara, Gandhi Memorial Museum etc....

 

Trichy –

Tiruchirappalli or Trichy, situated on the banks of the river Cauvery is the fourth largest city in Tamil Nadu.

It is a fine blend of tradition and modernity built around the Rock Fort

The city is a thriving commercial centre famous for artificial diamonds, cigars, handloom cloth, glass bangles and wooden and clay toys.

Major attractions: Ucchi Pillaiyar Temple, Tiruchirappalli Rock Fort ,Kallanai Dam, Art Museum, Sri Ranganathaswamy etc....

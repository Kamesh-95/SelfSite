Kanchipuram

Well known known for its 'Kanchipuram sarees' and also referred to as the 'golden city of thousand temples', Kanchipuram has various temples of religious and cultural importance spread all over the city.

Kanchipuram or just Kanchi also attracts a lot of tourists from all over the world who are interested in Hinduism or just want to enjoy the marvel of South Indian architecture and grandeur.

Sri Varadharaja Perumal Temple:

Varadharaja Perumal Temple or Hastagiri or Attiyuran is a famous vishnu located in the holy city of Kanchipuram.

It is one of the Divya Desams, the 108 temples of Vishnu believed to have been visited by the 12 poet saints, or Alwars. The temple is famous for its huge umbrella used during festive occasions.

Bhramotsavam is a major festival held in the Tamil month of Vaigasi (May-June).

 

Sri kanchi Kamakshi Amman Temple

It is a prominent ancient Hindu Temple dedicated to Kamakshi, one of the forms of Goddess Parvati, built by the Pallavas during 6 th Century AD.

Main Deity, Kamakshi, is seated in a majestic Padmasana, an yogic posture signifying peace and prosperity, holding sugarcane bow in the left upper arm, lotus and parrot in the right upper hand.


 

Sri Kailasanathar Temple

kanchi Kailasanathar temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is oldest temple in the town with Dravidian architectural style.

The low-slung sandstone compound of the temple contains a large number of carvings, including many half-animal deities which were popular during the early Dravidian architectural period.


 

Sri Kanchi Kamakoti Peetham

It is a Hindu monastic institution established Sri Adi Shankaracharya.

It dedicated to the worship of Kamakoti, referring to Goddess Durga.
